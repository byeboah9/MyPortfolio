About

The character for this Portfolio is Travis Bickle from the movie Taxi Driver(1976), directed by Martin Scorsese and written by Paul Schrader. The movie was critically acclaimed with it winning the prestigous Palm d'or and four Academy Award nominations, including Best Pictures and Best Leading Actor performances for Robert De Niro. Travis Bickle is meant to embodied the loniessness of men and combine with the cyanical/ dangerous Outside world can really mess with the human psyche much more easily than one would expect. This film has inspired generations of younger generations and films most particularly Joker(2019).

Background

Travis Bickle resides in New York City where he finds employment as a Taxi Driver, to curb his ever growing insomnia. It's stated that in the film he is a Vietnam War veteran and was in the Marine sector. A lot of Travis's condition of mental instablity to subtlely hinted as PSTD, which Travis hasn't recognize as such. In the beginning of the film it's to be said that Travis is single and has very lonely thoughts to stop this, however condicts himself sometimes by being a porno movie goer and being homestuck. Travis is relatively private with his life to the extend of hiding his address from his parents, and keeps his thought on his journal.

Character Arc

Travis Bickle is a complex character to analyze, however his intention throughtout the film are clear. He a man who is desperate to find someone to keep him company whether it be a girlfriend, co-worker, or a reliable person he can depend on. In the beginning of the film we know Travis's background and characteristics he still believe in a better future for him and his surrondings. When he first see Betsy for the first time Travis felt a potential of long-lasting love and comfort, especially needed in the harsh streets of NYC. It even translates with his support of presidential candidate Charles Palantine, as Betsy is a campaign worker for him. After a date gone wrong with Betsy and her seeming very uninterested with him, Travis is back to the same state as he was before, but now more angry and sad with his situation. He has no one to help or deeply talk to as his job is there to pay the bills, and his co-workers are more like acquintances. His motivate changes after an incident where a skipliy clad girl comes to after taxi out of nowhere and asked him to hurry and leave. However, she is stopped by a mysterious man where he gives Travis some money to compensate for the distrubance in his car, and takes the girl with him. Feeling a little complicit about doing nothing to help the girl, Travis works while trying to find the girl who was in his car wanting help. Eventually getting finding her location, he discovers that this young girl is a postitute and the man who took out of the car is her pimp. Disgusted with the siutation going on Travis is set on helping this young girl (named Iris)to escape her predictment. The two become somewhat friends and he made an agreement with her to give her money to left her job and move far away from there. Whilst this happens Travis is even more mental destroy with see what unmoralism seeing in front of his eyes, and feels that the people in power are there to blame, so decides to plan to kill Charles Palantine. When he failed to asssassinate him and luckliy escapes, and changes his plan to get Iris out of place by force.

Supporting Characters

Betsy

Betsy is played by actress Cybill Shepard and has already made her mark in the movie industry with films like The Last Picture Show (1970). Betsy is more of a mysterious character as the film doesn't give much information about herself. Despite having a short relationship with Travis, it's clearly an importance one for Travis and further emphasis the feeling of cynicism and loneliness. She works as a campaign worker for presidential candidate Charles Palantine and typically talks to her co-worker Tom. They both notice Travis as he drives his taxi consistency around the headquarters and makes Betsy a little uncomfortable. However, when Travis does ask her out it seems that she kind of has a little bit respect for him being up front about his feeling about her. Howver, after Travis diastously screws up the date for the both of them she firmly made the decision to not associate herself to Travis. After the date we rarely see her in film to further emphasize to distance of where her and Travis are at.

Iris

Iris Steensma is played by a Jodie Foster a role which attracted controversy initally due to the part of what her character is, and believing that it was inapporiate for a girl that young to be playing such a role. Nevertheless, Foster received her first Oscar nomination for Best Supporting Actress and believes that a role like this help formed her acting skills even more. Iris background is that she ran out from home and move to Manhattan. She was quickly picked up in the dark underbelly of the city get a job as an underage prositiute by a pimp named Sport. Now Sport and her have a relatively friendly and loving relationship (albeit Stockholm Syndrome), and Iris is quick to defend him, as she can't recollected a memory where she was abused by him. Travis first meets Iris when she comes to his taxi wanting to leave, but Sport catches her on time. With this experience affectong him deeply he eventually meet up with her, by posing as a customer with true intention of freeing her. With Travis trying to free Iris along the way they get to know each other a bit, where their conversations exposes Iris naivete by putting her logic with astronomy and not fully grasping the situation at hand. With this both made an agreement that Travis would send her money for her to move to a commune in Vermont. Their relationship is Travis's morality need to take action to put it in his own words "getting rid of dog, the filth". Him getting fed up with such deprave actions within his community.

Charles Palantine

Charles Palantine is played by Leonard Harris who only has two major film credit, with this movie being to most memorable from his short career. In the film Charles is a senator in New York and is making a promising campaign to run of the Oval Office, and has a big fanbase among to citizens in New York City. Due to Travis love for Betsy has a endlessly supported Palantine's campaign for president, despite not knowing any of his policies. One night working in the night shift Travis pick up Mr.Palantine himself along with his security going to a obligation. The conversation starts with Travis praising Palantine saying "You're great man everybody I know is gonna vote for you" ,then leads to Travis venting out his frustrations on the city. Palantine response was a vague but sucessful response to Travis triad where there needed to be radical changes, which Travis response "Damn straight". Once dropping off Palantine to his location Travis felt the need to say to Palantine that's he a good man and is sure he would win the race. This would be both entire interaction in the film, and it showcases Travis's desperation of fast change within the city of New York and uses Palantine as some sort of demigod to represent a better and moral future.
